Buche de Noel

Cake:

  1. 5 eggs
    • (separate yolks and whites)
  2. ¾ C sugar
  3. 1 tsp vanilla
  4. ¾ C cornstarch
  5. ¼ tsp baking powder
  6. 1 pinch of salt
Filling:
  • 1 C heavy cream
  • ¼ C sugar
Choose one of these for the filling:
  • ¼ C raspberry jam
  • 2 Tbsp frozen orange juice
  • ¼ Nutella
Frosting:
  • 1 C semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • ¼ C butter
  • ½ C powdered sugar

Preheat the oven at 350 F. Whisk the egg yolks with the sugar until nice and fluffy. Add the vanilla, cornstarch, baking powder and salt. Whisk the egg whites in a mixer until hard. Incorporate half of the white in the egg yolk mixture. Then fold the other half in. Line a cookie sheet with a buttered aluminum foil and spread the batter evenly onto it.

Bake at 350 degrees for 15 to 18 minutes. Put a dry towel on your counter and put the cake on it. Remove parchment paper. Roll the cake with the towel and let it completely cool. Prepare the filling by whisking the cream with the sugar until the whip cream is nice and hard. Add the filling of your choice to the whipped cream and stir. Unroll the cake and spread the whipped cream mixture evenly. Roll the cake. Melt the chocolate chips with the butter. Add the sugar and stir. Let it cool a little bit until a little thick and spread it evenly on the cake. Serve fresh.



Kiwi, Raspberry Swiss Roll

Cake:

  1. 5 eggs
    • (separate yolks and whites)
  2. ¾ C sugar
  3. 1 tsp vanilla
  4. ¾ C cornstarch
  5. ¼ tsp baking powder
  6. 1 pinch of salt
Filling:
  • 4 kiwis
  • 1/2 C of raspberry preserves
  • 1 ½ C heavy cream
  • ½ C sugar

Preheat the oven at 350 F. Whisk the egg yolks with the sugar until nice and fluffy. Add the vanilla, cornstarch, baking powder and salt. Whisk the egg whites in a mixer until hard. Incorporate half of the white in the egg yolk mixture. Then fold the other half in. Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper and spread the batter evenly onto it.

Bake at 350 degrees for 18 minutes. Put a dry towel on your counter and put the cake on it. Remove parchment paper. Roll the cake with the towel and let it completely cool. Prepare the filling by whisking the cream with the sugar until the whip cream is nice and hard. Peel and slice the kiwis in thin slices. Unroll the cake and spread the raspberry preserves onto it. Layer the kiwi slices onto the raspberry jam. Add ⅓ of the whipped cream and spread it evenly. Roll the cake back up and spread the rest of the whipped cream on the outside of the cake. Serve fresh.



Tomato Bisque

Ingredients:

  • ½ C onion - chopped
  • ½ C unsalted butter - divided
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed or minced
  • 1 ½ tsp dill weed
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp basil
  • ½ C onion - chopped
  • 5 C tomatoes, peeled, seeded and chopped (drain juice)
  • (may substitute canned diced tomatoes, or canned crushed tomatoes)
  • 1 6 oz can tomato paste
  • 4 C chicken stock
  • 2 Tbsp flour
  • 2 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp white pepper
  • 4 tsp honey
  • 1 ¼ cups heavy cream
  • ⅔ C half and half cream

In a large pot, melt 6 Tbsp butter. Saute onions, garlic, herbs on med-low for about 5 minutes or until onion is translucent. Add tomatoes, tomato paste and chicken stock and heat to simmer. Make a roux with the flour and remaining 2 Tbsp butter, whisking constantly over medium heat for 3 minutes, without browning.

Add roux to stock, whisking to blend. Add salt and pepper. Bring to a boil to thicken, stirring occasionally. Reduce heat to simmer, uncovered for 15 minutes. At this point you can puree it in a blender or leave it as it depends if you like your soup with chunks. At the end, add honey, cream and half and half.
